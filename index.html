North  Karnataka

Bagalkote

Bagalkote is a city in the Indian state of Karnataka, known for its rich cultural heritage and historical significance. It is famous for the Chalukya-era monuments at Badami, Aihole, and Pattadakal, which are UNESCO World Heritage Sites. The region is also well-known for its educational institutions and vibrant agricultural activities. Bagalkote plays a significant role in Karnataka's economy, contributing to the state's cement and sugar production industries.

Bijapur

Bijapur, officially known as Vijayapura, is a district in the Indian state of Karnataka. It is renowned for its historical significance and rich architectural heritage, including the famous Gol Gumbaz, which boasts one of the largest domes in the world. The district is a cultural hub with a blend of diverse traditions and festivals. Agriculture, especially the cultivation of sugarcane, pulses, and oilseeds, is the primary economic activity in Bijapur.

Gadag

Gadag is a district in the Indian state of Karnataka, known for its rich cultural and historical heritage. It features prominent architectural marvels such as the Trikuteshwara Temple and the Veeranarayana Temple, which showcase Chalukyan art and architecture. The district is also a center for the Kannada literary tradition, being the birthplace of many noted poets and scholars. Agriculture is a key part of Gadag's economy, with crops like jowar, groundnut, and cotton being widely cultivated.

Dharwad

Dharwad is a district in the Indian state of Karnataka, known for its educational institutions, including the prestigious Karnataka University and the University of Agricultural Sciences. The district has a rich musical heritage, being the birthplace of many classical musicians like Pandit Bhimsen Joshi. Dharwad is also famous for its unique sweet delicacy, Dharwad Peda. The economy is driven by agriculture, education, and small-scale industries.

Belagavi

It is known for its diverse cultural influences and historical landmarks, including the Belgaum Fort and the Kamal Basti Jain temple. Bellary

Bellary, officially known as Ballari, is a district in the Indian state of Karnataka. It is renowned for its rich mineral resources, especially iron ore, making it a significant hub for mining and steel production. The district is home to the historic Bellary Fort and the UNESCO World Heritage Site Hampi, which boasts stunning Vijayanagara Empire ruins. Bidar

The district is famous for the Bidar Fort, a marvel of Persian architecture, and the Bahmani tombs. It is also known for its traditional Bidriware, a unique metal handicraft. Kalaburagi

It is renowned for its historical monuments, including the Gulbarga Fort and the Jama Masjid, which reflect the region's rich Islamic architecture. The district is an important center for education, hosting institutions like Gulbarga University and the Central University of Karnataka. Koppal

The district is home to the ancient Mahadeva Temple at Itagi and the Anegundi village, which is considered the cradle of the Vijayanagara Empire. Koppal is also famous for its traditional crafts, including the making of religious statues and idols. Uttar Karnataka

Uttar Karnataka, also known as North Karnataka, is a region in the Indian state of Karnataka, encompassing several districts including Bagalkot, Belagavi, Bijapur, Dharwad, Gadag, Haveri, and others. This region is known for its historical and architectural treasures, such as the ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire in Hampi and the intricate temples of Badami and Aihole. It is also recognized for its rich cultural heritage, including classical music and traditional crafts. Agriculture is a significant part of the economy, with major crops including cotton, jowar, and sugarcane.

South Karnataka

Bangalore

Bengaluru, also known as Bangalore, is the capital city of Karnataka and one of the major districts in the state. It is renowned for being India's tech hub, with numerous IT companies and startups based in the city. Bengaluru is also known for its vibrant cultural scene, diverse cuisine, and numerous parks and green spaces. The city's economy is bolstered by its status as a major center for education, research, and aerospace industries.

Shimoga

Shimoga, officially known as Shivamogga, is a district in the Indian state of Karnataka, located in the central part of the state. It is known for its lush green landscapes, including the scenic Jog Falls, one of the highest waterfalls in India. Shimoga is also recognized for its rich biodiversity, with several wildlife sanctuaries and forested areas. Agriculture is a major economic activity, with crops like rice, arecanut, and coffee being prominently cultivated. The district also has a vibrant cultural scene and historical significance.

Udupi

Udupi is a district in the southern part of Karnataka, renowned for its coastal beauty and cultural heritage. It is famous for its Udupi Krishna Temple, a significant pilgrimage site, and its distinctive Udupi cuisine, known for its vegetarian dishes. The district features beautiful beaches, such as Malpe and St. Mary's Island, and is an important center for education and healthcare. Udupi’s economy is supported by agriculture, tourism, and the fishing industry.

Chickmagalur

Chickmagalur, also known as Chikmagalur, is a district in the southern part of Karnataka, known for its picturesque landscapes and coffee plantations. It is often referred to as the "Coffee Land of Karnataka" due to its extensive coffee-growing estates. The district features lush greenery, scenic hills, and prominent attractions such as the Mullayanagiri Peak, the highest point in Karnataka, and the Kudremukh National Park. Chickmagalur is also recognized for its pleasant climate and is a popular destination for nature lovers and trekkers.

Tumakuru

Tumakuru, also known as Tumkur, is a district in the central part of Karnataka. It is known for its agricultural activities, with crops like jowar, sugarcane, and groundnut being major products. The district features several historical and cultural sites, including the Siddaganga Matha, a prominent religious and educational institution. Tumakuru is also a growing urban center with industries and educational institutions contributing to its development. The district is strategically located, acting as a bridge between North and South Karnataka.

Dakshina Kannada

Dakshina Kannada is a district in the southern part of Karnataka, known for its beautiful coastal region along the Arabian Sea. The district is famous for its vibrant culture, historic temples, and lush landscapes. Key attractions include the coastal town of Mangalore, the Kadri Manjunath Temple, and the beautiful beaches of Ullal and Someshwar. Dakshina Kannada's economy is driven by agriculture, particularly the cultivation of arecanut, coffee, and spices, as well as by industries and commerce.

Hassan

Hassan is a district in the southern part of Karnataka, known for its rich historical and cultural heritage. It features significant architectural sites, including the Hoysala temples in Belur and Halebidu, which are renowned for their intricate carvings and sculptures. The district is also known for its lush green landscapes and coffee plantations. Hassan serves as an important center for agriculture, with major crops including paddy, coffee, and sugarcane.

Mandya

Mandya is a district in the southern part of Karnataka, known for its agricultural significance and historical sites. It is often referred to as the "Sugar Bowl of Karnataka" due to its extensive sugarcane cultivation. Mandya features attractions such as the Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary, which is a haven for migratory birds, and the historical town of Srirangapatna with its heritage sites. The district also has a vibrant cultural scene and is an important center for regional festivals and traditions.

Ramanagara

Ramanagara is a district in the southern part of Karnataka, known for its scenic landscapes and agricultural activities. It is famous for the Ramanagara hills, which are popular for trekking and rock climbing. The district also has historical significance with the presence of ancient temples and forts. Ramanagara's economy is primarily driven by agriculture, with crops like cotton, ragi, and sugarcane being prominent. Additionally, it is known for its silk industry, contributing to Karnataka's reputation as a major silk producer.

Kolar

Kolar is a district in the southern part of Karnataka, known for its historical significance and agricultural activities. The district is famous for the Kolar Gold Fields, which were once one of the deepest and most productive gold mines in India. Kolar is also home to important religious sites such as the Kolaramma Temple and the Bhoga Nandeeshwara Temple. Agriculture plays a key role in the local economy, with crops like ragi, groundnut, and sunflower being widely cultivated.

Kodagu

Kodagu, also known as Coorg, is a district in the southwestern part of Karnataka, renowned for its scenic beauty and coffee plantations. It is famous for its lush green hills, misty landscapes, and significant coffee production, earning it the nickname "Scotland of India." Key attractions include the Abbey Falls, Raja's Seat, and the Namdroling Monastery. Kodagu is also known for its unique culture, including traditional Kodava cuisine and festivals. Agriculture, particularly coffee and spices, is a major economic activity in the district. widely cultivated.

Mysuru

Mysuru, also known as Mysore, is a district in the southern part of Karnataka, renowned for its historical and cultural significance. The city of Mysuru, the district's headquarters, is famous for the Mysore Palace, a stunning example of Indo-Saracenic architecture, and the annual Dasara festival, a major cultural event. Mysuru is also known for its traditional silk sarees, Mysore Pak (a popular sweet), and its educational institutions. The district has a rich heritage, lush gardens, and a significant role in Karnataka's tourism and cultural landscape.

Chamarajanagar

Chamarajanagar is a district in the southern part of Karnataka, known for its natural beauty and historical sites. The district is home to the Bandipur National Park and the Biligiri Ranganath Temple, both of which are notable for their ecological and cultural significance. Chamarajanagar's economy is largely based on agriculture, with crops like sugarcane, maize, and pulses being cultivated. The district is also known for its unique blend of wildlife, pilgrimage tourism, and local traditions.
